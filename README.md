# JOSL - Jasper Open Standard License

**Open for everyone. Protected from fragmentation. One standard, unified evolution.**

```
Version: 1.0
Author:  Jasper van de Meent
Year:    2025
Status:  Active
```

---

## Purpose

JOSL enables **free use, study, and implementation** of the JTel Identity Standard and related technologies while maintaining:

- The **integrity** of the standard
- The **authorship** of its creator
- A **unified and consistent evolution** of the specification

JOSL ensures JIS remains an **open standard** without fragmenting into competing variants.

---

## Applies To

- **JIS** (JTel Identity Standard)
- **Humotica**
- **BETTI** (Balanced Economic Transaction Trust Integration)
- **TIBET** (Time-based Intent BETTI Exchange Token)
- **FIR/A** (First Initiation Revoke/Accept)
- **SNAFT** (System Not Authorized For That)
- **BALANS** (Pre-execution Decision Engine)
- **NIR** (Notify, Identify, Rectify)
- **HICSS** (Human Interrupt Control System)
- **HID/DID** (Human/Device Identity)
- All documentation and reference implementations

---

## Permissions (You May)

- **Use** - Read, study, and reference the specifications
- **Implement** - Build in open-source software, commercial products, IoT devices, robotics, telecom, identity frameworks, operating systems
- **Integrate** - Layer JIS on top of existing protocols (SIP, HTTP, MQTT, Matrix, WebRTC, etc.)
- **Extend** - Propose extensions through the official JIS RFC process
- **Commercialize** - Build and sell products using JIS

---

## Requirements (You Must)

- **Attribution** - Display the required attribution (see below)
- **Preserve Naming** - Do not rename the standard or publish forks as "official"
- **Preserve License** - Keep the JOSL text intact and included
- **Declare Non-Conformance** - If your implementation deviates, state it clearly

---

## Prohibitions (You May Not)

- **Claim Ownership** - You cannot claim to own the JIS specification
- **Privatize** - You cannot relicense or close-source any part of JIS
- **Mislead** - You cannot publish incompatible derivatives as "JIS"
- **Remove Authorship** - You cannot change or hide the origin
- **Create Competing Governance** - You cannot create alternative "official JIS" organizations

---

## Required Attribution

When using or implementing JIS, you must clearly display:

```
"Powered by JIS (JTel Identity Standard), authored by Jasper van de Meent."
```

---

## Non-Conformance Declaration

If your implementation deviates from the official spec, you must state:

```
"This implementation is not fully conformant with the official JIS standard."
```
